Physics

What is Physics?


Physics is a wonderful subject that marries the mind of a child with nature and its wonders.

It is difficult, if not impossible, to define the boundaries of Physics which is vast and ever changing.

In simple words, Physics is the branch of science concerned with the natural phenomena.

These natural phenomena include mechanics, heat, light and other radiation, sound, electricity, magnetism, and the structure of atoms.

What does Physics require?


As NEET aspirants, who major in Biology, Physics can be intimidating, and people often want to know about the prerequisites to start learning physics.

What worsens this phobia is the fact that Physics requires some Math.

But please understand, you don't need to know every math idea in algebra and trigonometry, and you can learn a lot of it along the way, but the more math that's foreign to you the likelier it is that physics might look like mathematical witchcraft.

Remember, the math isn't there to serve as some sort of intellectual roadblock to prevent people from understanding. The role of math here is to state a physics concept in a concise and clear way.

Try not to let the math obscure the basic and, usually simple, physics concept that lies underneath.

More important than existing math knowledge, is your mindset. No matter how much you prepare, you'll come up against roadblocks.

The key to being successful is knowing you can work hard and ultimately overcome these obstacles!


Role of Physics in NEET & Medicine


The role of Physics in NEET is to test the aptitude and reasoning ability of the aspirant. Physics is actually used to check the imagination of the candidate , therefore resulting in selection of the smarter candidates.

And I know some of you would even question the relation between Physics and medicine and call it useless and a huge time waste but, Physics actually has a lot to do with medecine-
  1. There is a separate branch called as biophysics which is studied in medical field.
  2. Physics is used in Radio-diagnosis.
  3. Physics is used in Nuclear Medicine.
  4. Physics is used in Biochemistry ( Modern Physics ).
These are just a few examples of the use of Physics in medicine.

The division of science into Physics, Chemistry and Biology is just for easier understanding of science and serves no purpose in the real world.
