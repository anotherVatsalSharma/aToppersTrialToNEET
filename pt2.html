Chapter 2: ELECTROSTATIC POTENTIAL AND CAPACITANCE


2.1 Introduction

The potential difference between two points in an electric field may be defined as the amount of work done in moving a unit positive charge from one point to the other against the electrostatic forces.

S.I. units of potential difference is Volts (V) named after Alessandro Volta

The potential difference between two points is said to be 1 V if 1 J of work has to be done in order to move a positive charge of 1 C from one point to another against the electrostatic forces.

2.2 Electric Potential


Electric potential at a point is the amount of work done in moving a unit positive charge from infinity to that point against the electrostatic forces.

The electric potential at a point in an electric field is said to be 1 V if 1 J of work is done in moving a unit positive charge from infinity to that point against the electrostatic forces.

2.9 ELECTROSTATICS OF CONDUCTORS

2.9.1 Conductors

These are substances which allow large scale physical movement of electric charges through them when an external electric field is applied.

2.9.2 Insulators

These are substances which don't allow large scale physical movement of electric charges through them when an external electric field is applied. 

2.9.3 Behavior of conductors in external electric field

1) Net electric field is zero inside the conductor
2) Just outside the surface of a charged conductor, electric field is normal to the surface.
3) The net electric charge in the interior is zero and any excess charge resides on its surface.
4) Potential is constant within and on the surface of the conductor
5) Electric field at the surface is proportional to the surface charge density

2.9.4 Electrostatic shielding

The phenomenon of making a region free from any electric field is called electrostatic shielding. It is based on the fact that electric field vanishes inside the cavity of a hollow conductor.
  

2.10 DIELECTRICS AND POLARIZATION

2.10.1 Dielectric

A dielectric is a substance which does not allow the flow of charges through it but permits them to exert electrostatic forces on one another through it. It is essentially an insulator.

A dielectric is an insulator which can be polarized through small localized displacements of its charges.

2.10.2 Polarization of dielectrics

In a conductor, the external electric field Eₒ moves the free charge carriers inducing electric field in the opposite direction to Eₒ . This process continues till the two fields cancel out eachother and the net electric field is zero.

In dielectrics, the external field Eₒ induces dipole moment by stretching or re-orienting the molecules of the dielectric. The induced dipole moment sets up an electric field which opposes Eₒ  but does not exactly cancel this field. It only reduces it.

2.10.2.1 Polar dielectrics and their polarization

A molecule in which the center of mass of positive charges (protons) does not coincide with the center of mass of negative charges (electrons) is called a polar molecule.

In the absence of any external electric field the dipole moments of different molecules are randomly oriented due to thermal agitation. The total dipole moment is zero.

When external electric field is applied to dipole moments of different molecules into line with the electric field. As a result there is a net dipole moment in the direction of electric field.

The extent of this polarization depends on the relative values of two opposing energies :
1) the potential energy of dipole in the external field
2) the thermal energy of agitation

2.10.2.2 Non-polar dielectrics and their polarization

A molecule in which the center of mass of positive charges coincides with the center of mass of negative charges is called in non polar molecule.

In the absence of any electric field centers of positive and negative charges for inside the dipole moment of each molecule is zero.

In the presence of external electric field the centers of positive charges are displaced in the direction of the electric field and the centers of negative charges are displaced in the opposite direction.

The displacement of charges stops when the force exerted on them by the external field is  balanced by a restoring force due to internal fields.

The induced dipole moments of different molecules add up tgiving net dipole moment to dielectric in the direction of the external field.

Hence both polar and non-polar dielectrics develop a net dipole moment in the presence of an external electric field. This is called polarization of dielectric.


The polarization is defined as the dipole moment per unit volume and its magnitude is usually referred to as the polarization density the direction of polarization is same as that of the external electric field.


2.10.3 Reduction of electric field by polarization of a dielectric

The positive charges of the dipoles of first vertical column cancel out the negative charges of second column and so on.

Thus the volume charge density in the interior of the slab is zero.

However, there is a net uncancelled negative charge on the left face and net uncancelled positive charge on the right face of the slab.

The polarized dielectric is equivalent to two charged surfaces with induced charge densities of ±σ.

Hence the resultant field is Eₒ - Eₐ < Eₒ

The ratio of original field to the reduced field is dielectric constant.

2.10.6 Dielectric strength

When a dielectric is placed in a very high electric field due out the electrons detached from the parent atoms.

The dielectric behaves like a conductor.

This phenomena is called dielectric breakdown.

The maximum electric field that can exist in a dielectric without causing the breakdown of its insulating property is called dielectric strength of that material.


2.11 CAPACITORS AND CAPACITANCE

2.11.1 Electrical Capacitance of a conductor

The electrical capacitance of a conductor is the measure of its ability to hold electric charge.

The capacitance of a capacitor may be defined as the charge required to increase the potential of the conductor by unit amount.

It depends upon :-
1) size and shape
2) nature of the surrounding medium
3) presence of other conductors

Capacitance of an isolated spherical conductor is 

C = 4πεₒ R

2.11.2 Concept of capacitors and it's principle

A capacitor is an arrangement of two conductors separated by an insulating material that is used to store electric charge and electric energy

Principle :

The capacitance of an insulated conductor is considerably increased when we place an earthed conductor near it. This system is called capacitor


Capacitance of a capacitor = C = Q / V

where Q = charge on either conductor and V = potential difference between them


The capacitance of a capacitor may be defined as the charge required to be supplied to either of the conductors so as to increase the potential difference between them by unit amount.


A capacitor has capacitance of 1 F if 1 C of charge is transferred from its one conductor to another on applying a potential difference of 1 V across the two conductors.
